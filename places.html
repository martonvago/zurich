Discover Hidden Gems

The Opera House

VK on Flickr

The Zürich Opera House has garnered worldwide acclaim for its outstanding acoustics and wide variety of international performances. The concert hall maintains its original Neo-classical façade, designed by Viennese architects F. Fellner and H. Helmer and held up by 1,800 oak pillars. Viator

Box office opening hours:

Mon–Sat: 11am–6pm or curtain up

Sun: Opens before curtain up

Ticket prices:

Evening: CHF 30–380

Afternoon: CHF 18–60

Rieterpark

Wikimedia

The 70,000 m2 Rieterpark is Zürich's largest and most impressive landscaped garden and is set in the district of Zürich Enge near Lake Zürich. The park and associated villa (now the Rietberg Museum) was created in 1855 and acquired in the late 19th century by the industrialist Rieter family from Winterthur. On a clear day you can see as far as the Glarus Alps, which conveys the impression of an almost endless vista. My Switzerland

Opening hours:

Mon–Sun: Open 24 hours

Grossmünster

Life Channel

The Grossmünster is a 12th-century Romanesque ex-cathedral with a fascinating history of Catholic saints and Protestant preaching. According to legend, the Grossmünster was founded by Charlemagne after his horse stopped over the graves of three early Christian martyrs. Sacred Destinations

Opening hours:

Mar–Oct: 10am–6pm

Nov–Feb: 10am–5pm

On Sundays: Opens after the service

Tower entry price:

Adults: CHF 5

Concessions: CHF 2

Kunsthaus Zürich

On the Grid

Zürich’s Kunsthaus contains over 4,000 works of art and is the best place in the country to explore Swiss artists. Alongside the homegrown talent, the Kunthaus features plenty of works by world-famous artists as well. A few hours here would not be wasted. Culture Trip

Opening hours:

Mon: Closed

Tue, Fri–Sun: 10am–6pm

Wed, Thu: 10am–8pm

Entry price:

Adults: CHF 26

Concessions: CHF 19

Under-17s & members: Free
