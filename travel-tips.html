An Insider Perspective

A Medley of Languages

Switzerland has four official languages: German, French, Italian and Romansh. Zürich lies in the German speaking part of the country, where people speak Swiss German, a German dialect, which differs from region to region. For anyone who speaks or is learning to speak German, this can be tiring because at first it‘s very hard to understand even for people with a German mother tongue. However, a lot of Swiss speak English fairly well, especially the younger generation, so getting by in English is generally no problem. In Your Pocket

Zürich on a Budget

With sit-down restaurants costing around CHF 40 per meal per person, eating out in Switzerland can be very costly, so buy your groceries. A week’s worth of food (bread, pasta, rice, eggs, vegetables, cheese, deli meats for sandwiches, and some assorted fruit) will cost you CHF 75–100. The major supermarkets are Migros, COOP, and Spar. COOP is the most expensive. Nomadic Matt

Lazy Sundays

Like in a lot of European countries, Sunday is a family day in Switzerland. In Zürich (and in most other towns in Switzerland) almost everything is closed on Sunday. But shops and restaurants in train stations and airports as well as some museums, cafés and bars will remain open. Spend Life Traveling

Staying Connected

The standard portable system (GSM/GPRS/3G) covers even the mountain regions well. However, Switzerland is not a member of the EU, so don't assume low call and data rates. In the event that you plan to remain for some time, you should think about obtaining a prepaid card. The markets offer them at very sensible costs. The nation code for Switzerland is +41, the zone code for Zürich is 44. Travel Triangle

Seeing a Doctor

The standard of Swiss healthcare is very high and ranked as the third best health system in the world. Foreign residents or tourists wanting to visit a doctor in Switzerland will typically need some form of insurance coverage. Those from the European Union (EU) or the European Economic Area (EEA) in Switzerland on a stay of less than three months can use their European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) to cover some of the costs.
